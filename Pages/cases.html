Cases Before and After Roper v. Simmons

James Terry Roach

[South Carolina Department of Corrections]

Before the death penalty was abolished, juveniles who committed cruel crimes were sentenced to death. James Terry Roach, murdering two teenagers at the age of 17, was the second person to be executed by the state of South Carolina.

“On December 13, 1977, Roach pleaded guilty as a principal to two counts of murder, criminal sexual conduct, armed robbery, and kidnapping” ((“James Terry Roach, Appellant, v. Warden Joseph Martin, Central Correctional Institutioncolumbia, South Carolina,Andt. Travis Medlock, Attorney General for South Carolina, Appellees, 757 F.2d 1463 (4th Cir. 1985)”).

Thomas Taylor and Carlotta Hartness, kidnapped by Roach, were each shot in the head and shot multiple times after being raped. According to the witnesses, Roach showed no signs of remorse and was arrested two days later to confess his crime. Regarding his brutal murder, Roach was sentenced guilty and was executed by electric chair at the age of 25 (January 10, 1986).

“...relating to mitigating circumstances, the sentencing judge considered that Roach was suffering from mental retardation and an antisocial personality disorder and was below the age of 18 at the time of the crime… Considering the circumstances in mitigation as well as the existence of the three statutory aggravating circumstances, the sentencing judge found as an affirmative fact that the evidence in the case warranted imposition of the death penalty upon Roach” ((“James Terry Roach, Appellant, v. Warden Joseph Martin, Central Correctional Institutioncolumbia, South Carolina,Andt. Travis Medlock, Attorney General for South Carolina, Appellees, 757 F.2d 1463 (4th Cir. 1985)”).

Terry Berger-Smith

[WSVN]

After the death penalty was prohibited in the United States, juveniles instead were sentenced to the juvenile hall, probation, juvenile detention center, blended sentence, or adult jail if the juvenile had committed a felony. Terry Berger-Smith, a 14-year-old boy was sentenced to more than 40 years in prison.

On June 10, 2022, Terry Berger-Smith, attacked, kidnapped, and sexually assaulted a 21-year-old woman, in Hollywood, Florida. Terry Beger-Smith allegedly forced her into secluded areas to perform sexual acts under threats of violence. Surveillance footage, witness testimony, and forensic evidence supported the victim's account. A key witness, Daniel Hernandez, identified Berger-Smith and revealed receiving a FaceTime call during the assault. The suspect also recorded parts of the crime and threatened to share it online. Berger-Smith faces charges of sexual battery and kidnapping.
